Prayer

From the records of June 2017.

Father, as we consider Your words, as we meditate on Your sayings.

We consider that You paid the price.
You paid the price that You were not required to.
You did it for us.

And if we counted it as nothing, something slights, just a common thing, cheap grace and trample Your blood under our feet, Lord, we fail to recognize the precious value of Your cross.

If we convince ourselves, Jesus suffered so I don't have to, Jesus experienced the agony so I can enjoy my best life now, then we make a mockery of Your grace. Because we are unwilling to count the cost lest we suffer any inconvenience and following You.

But Lord, Your cross is not merely inconvenient, it requires everything. It means death, death to our self life, death to our selfish desires and passions, death to carnal thoughts and feelings, death to our will for rebellion against holy God.

And if we resist this cross, if we refuse to take up this cross, then it means we're unwilling to die the self, we are unwilling to put to death our rebellion against you, we are unwilling to cease in our carnal thoughts and desires.

And if that be true then how dare we say that we believe in You?
You who paid the perfect price.
You who paid the ultimate costs in agony with Your own blood.
How dare we cheapen Your grace?

And Lord, it is no small wonder than that You tell us we cannot be Your disciples. For than we make ourselves unworthy of the great name, Christian.

Lord, forgive us.
We repent.
And we take up that cross be. We put it on our shoulders. We lift it upon our back.
We acknowledge our guilt before holy God.
We accept to the penalty for our long actions, death.

As followers of the one who died for us, we nevertheless find that we live.
We died but will live.
We deny ourselves.
We crucified with Christ.
We gladly identify with crucified Christ, so that we might identify with risen Christ.
We take up our cross and follow You, as You carry Your cross. Then we might have the privilege of being crucified with You.
We identify with the thief on the cross who found salvation by being crucified with You.

And Lord, from this point forth, we resolve to follow You.
We count the cost. We consider the price that it'll cost everything. And yet this small price that we pay, even though we cost everything, is nothing compare to the glory that we will receive and identifying with the King of kings, the King of the universe.

So Lord we say thank You for the privilege following You.
Thank You for the privilege being crucified with You.
Thank you that You identified with our death so we might identify with Your resurrection.
Thank you for the privilege being called the disciple.
Thank you for the honor being called the real Christian.
And we offer ourselves as living sacrifices.
